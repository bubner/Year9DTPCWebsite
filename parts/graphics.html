INFORMATION 
            The graphics processing unit, or GPU, is the small chip inside a graphics card responsible for image
            rendering on the computer. GPUs are also sometimes referred to as video cards or graphics cards.
            Every PC has a GPU that is used to show pictures, videos, and 2D or 3D animations. Quick math
            computations are handled by a GPU, which frees up the CPU for other tasks. A GPU, which is present
            in the greatest gaming graphics cards, includes thousands of tiny cores designed for multitasking,
            as opposed to a CPU's few cores that are focused on sequential serial processing.
