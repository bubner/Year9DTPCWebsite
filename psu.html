INFORMATION 
                To ensure the proper operation of components within a computer, the Power Supply Unit (PSU) is a piece of hardware that transforms
                outlet power into useable power. In order to use low-voltage regulated DC power for internal computer components, it specifically
                transforms mains AC into lower voltage DC current. Additionally, it manages overheating by controlling voltage. The power supply
                is crucial, to put it simply. Without a power supply PC, other internal components will not operate correctly as they would have
                unregulated electricity flowing through them. The power supply device is often found at the rear of the computer casing.
DESCRIPTION
